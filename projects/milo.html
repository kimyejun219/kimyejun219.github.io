milo

problem/goal

The goal for this project was to take an everyday object, expand on the characteristics of the object, and redefine it. I chose to expand on the idea of an alarm clock.

research

I personally am very sensitive to sound and always get startled by the sound of alarms or any ringing noises from a clock, phone, or any kinds of alarming device. In a small survey by a British magazine company, 53% of people feel dreadful and 27% anxious when their alarm goes off. sound, in the form of an alarm, is definitely not a pleasing tone to hear. on the contrary to sound, light is often used as a therapeutic source (quite literally). I wanted to explore an opportunity here: how can i replace an unattractive component of sound and replace it with light to alert and send information?

the idea

milo is a clock, specifically an alarm clock that speaks with light, not numbers or sounds. For people with sensitive hearing or in a focused and quiet environment, milo allows tracking of time without startling and alarming noises. Accommodating to the environment, at daytime, the white light is shown to focus on brightness and awareness. At night, the light becomes warmer, to ease the eyes and allow relaxation. The clock signals when the button is pressed (alarm is instantiated) and when the counting time is over.

how was it made?

iteration 1: the first iteration simply focused on the functional side of the product. The convenience of pressing a button at the very top rather than the sides, having space to store the arduino board, and creating opaque corners to hide the wires. As i assembled and tried using this iteration, all the functionality was there, but i felt that the button press and the lamp itself was a little disconnected.

iteration 2: to modify the feeling of disconnect, i wondered, ‘what if the light itself is just a one big button?’ I removed the wooden corners that hid the wires (because i did not need them functionally) and made space between the arduino board and the light to incorporate the button mechanism.

what's next?

for future improvements, i asked three questions:
- can we give light physical properties?
- what if the lamp responds to you?
- how can lamp show more information?

fortunately, i had the opportunity to explore these questions in my next project, mimo. check it out!
