community farmer's market map application

team members

vikram kumar, david neil daniel, abigail gutierrez-ray, and yejun kim

problem/goal

our goal for the community farmer’s market was to find a way to increase consumer to vendor communication so that vendors can benefit from consumer feedback.

research

we initially went to the Grant Park Farmer’s Market to get some ideas as to what our problem space could be. After talking to Myriam, owner of Raw Head Bread, we got the indication that vendors and consumers had virtually no ideal method to communicate. As a whole, we observed that vendors had to hand out their email manually to communicate with consumers, consumers did not have an easy way to communicate with vendors without verbally speaking to them, and communication between vendors and consumers was lacking in such way that hindered vendor growth.


some existing resources for communication:
google reviews: vendors are able to see and respond to comments/concerns regarding their product. This resource is also impersonal, requires intentional effort on the customer side, which could discourage interaction of the platform and deprive the vendor of feedback. customer is also not directly responding to the vendor but rather the product, which can facilitate ineffective feedback.
facebook: facebook groups allow vendors to communicate with each other. the current CFM facebook group is not used as a communication of engagement platform, but rather to hire new workers or post general announcements. This resource is weak as it has allowed for inactivity and requires regular engagement on all vendor parts in order to be an effective form of communication.
square: vendors are able to indirectly interact with consumers through sales data. This model breaks when it comes to consumers who do not buy regularly or show interest but do not make a purchase. This platform allows for gaps in assessment of consumer tastes because it does not provide any feedback regarding the consumers opinions of the product.

the idea

we came up with three ways to encourage involvement of both stakeholders and increase communication between them:

  • a list of farmers markets to search for vendors
  • provide an interactive map for customers to easily locate vendors during, before, or after the market.
  • allow customers the opportunity to fill out feedback forms and leave ratings that the vendors can review.

wireframe

personas:

storyboards: we created storyboards to visualize possible interactions the stakeholders could have.

ui sketches: after deciding that a mobile application would be the most optimal solution, we sketched out possible UI’s representing the solution areas.

low-fi prototype: after our low-fi prototype creation, we went through a cognitive walkthrough evaluation.

high-fi prototype:

Landing page for users to sign in.

Upon signing in, the user is taken directly to the map view of their "home" market

Map view allows for users to "pinch" to zoom in or out of the market layout.

Map view becomes more detailed as user zooms in.

Users can navigate to the market selection from the map view.

The drop-down menu reflect the changes in the location of the markets by season.

Search bar at the top of the map view allows for users familiar with the market to navigate more quickly.

Vendor location is highlighted upon searching.

Each vendor has their own contact card which displays ratings, number of favorites, and views.

Upon scrolling down the vendor's info card, user is able to connect with the vendor.

The bottom menu bar allows for quick access to the user's selected favorite vendors

The profile page allows access to notifications and messages sent between consumers and vendors.

The feedback features allows vendors to easily view comments and ratings.
